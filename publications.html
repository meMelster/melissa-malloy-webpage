Bilingual Sentiment Analysis of Spanglish Tweets

There is a huge gap in sentiment classification in English versus other languages. In this work I used Python's NLTK library and applied Naive Bayes classifier to Spanglish tweets. I Designed an algorithm for preprocessing Spanglish text using POS tagging, stemming, and other methods in Spanglish to train a model and prove that experiments using this model will achieve a higher F-Score, as oppose to using a single lexical dictionary.

Sentiment Analysis of Twitter, SMS, and Other Short Text

A practical survey of challenges and solutions regarding the brevity and informality that these popular texts pose for sentiment analysis. In this paper, I discuss sentiment analysis of various types of informally written short texts along with different approaches for feature reduction and preprocessing, sentiment classification, and analysis of entire phrases within messages. In addition, I briefly discuss a few successful sentiment analysis APIs.

Empowerment with Informal Learning

An application of mobile technology to teach computer science in grades K-12, was presented at Micro2015, the 2nd International Conference of Microelectronics, Circuits and Systems. In this initiative I teamed up with the Children's Services Council of Broward County to increase middle school students' interest in STEM programs. This was achieved from their participation in an after-school program, which allowed them to be actively involved in the personalization of an Android App.
